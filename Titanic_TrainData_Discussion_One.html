My personal analysis: Interesting initial observations: Can see from the descriptive stats above that the majority of Titanic passengers did not survive (mean is less than 0.5). The majority of passengers were in third class as demonstrated by the mean being 2.31, as well as the 50% quartile (median) being 3. Also, the mean fare price was $32.20, which seems like a lot of money for 1912. A quick check for a general inflation reference on:www.officialdata.org/us/inflation/1912?amount=32.20

indicates that the mean ticket would be approximately $851.12 or so today, which is definitely a sizeable sum for a cross-Atlantic cruise or even flight, in 2020. To put in even further context, this fare price is the mean, which is again tilted towards second and third classes, meaning that it is likely second and first class passengers paid much more for their ticket than this sum.

Also interesting is the frequency of people traveling with immediate family members (SibSp), it seems most traveled alone (50% quartile) but some traveled with many members.
